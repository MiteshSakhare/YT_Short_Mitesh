# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
Whispers rippled through the gallery. The human judge’s eyes narrowed. “You are not here to discuss Captain Valerius’s actions.”

“I am here to establish context,” Kaelen replied. “An accusation of heresy cannot be separated from the political climate that produced it. I was marked without my consent by a Church agent. I was targeted by an armed squad before any investigation was conducted. And now I stand before a tribunal that includes the very faction that has already declared me guilty.” He looked directly at the masked inquisitor. “Tell me, Inquisitor. When did the Church decide I was a heretic? Was it when my mana tested unusual? Or was it when I refused to be taken in chains?”

The chamber went silent. The masked inquisitor’s gloved hands tightened on the armrests of his chair.

The human judge cleared her throat. “The tribunal is not here to debate Church procedures. We are here to determine whether you pose a threat to the Academy’s truce. Evidence will be presented. Witnesses will be called. You will have your chance to speak.”

She gestured to a side door, and Captain Valerius entered, his scarred face flushed with righteous anger. He took a position opposite Kaelen, his hand resting on the hilt of his ceremonial sword.

“Captain Valerius,” the human judge said. “You initiated the investigation into the accused. Present your evidence.”
