# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
Seraphina’s eyes widened. “Rin, if you do this, you’ll be openly aligned with him. With us. There’s no going back.”

“There’s no going back for any of us.” Rin’s grin was fierce. “I made my choice the first night I tasted him.” She looked at Kaelen, and her voice softened. “I’ll be back before the hour’s up. Don’t do anything stupid while I’m gone.”

She vanished into the tunnels, her footsteps fading into silence.

Elara looked up from her books. “That leaves Seraphina’s key. The bloodline lock. It’s tied to her life force. Only she can remove it.”

Seraphina’s hand tightened on Kaelen’s. “I know.”

“It will hurt,” Elara said quietly. “Breaking a bloodline bond always hurts. And when it’s broken, you won’t be able to remake it. Not ever.”

Seraphina was silent for a moment. Then she turned to Kaelen, her golden eyes steady. “When I was a child, my grandmother used to tell me about the pact. She said it was a promise—a promise that the Demon Emperor would return and give us what we were owed. I grew up thinking you were a monster. A weapon. A thing to be used.” She reached up and touched his face, her palm warm against his cheek. “I was wrong.”

She leaned in and kissed him—soft, slow, a goodbye and a beginning all at once. When she pulled back, there were tears in her eyes, but her voice was steady.
