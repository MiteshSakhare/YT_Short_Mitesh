# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
“I’m going to give them what they want,” he said. “A heretic. A threat. A reason to move.” He closed his hand around the key. “And when they come for me, I’m going to show them exactly what I am.”

Rin grinned, her orange eyes blazing. “Now you’re talking.”

Seraphina stepped forward, her hand closing over his fist. “If you do this, there’s no going back. The limiter comes off. The seal opens. And whatever you become…” She met his eyes. “You don’t come back from that.”

“Are you ready?”

He looked at her hand on his, at Rin’s eager smile, at Elara’s steady gaze. He thought of the frozen ocean within him, the chains of light, the darkness coiled beneath. He thought of the prophecy: When the twice-crowned king returns, the bonds of blood shall break and burn.

He opened his hand, the key glinting in the firelight.

The plan was simple. That was what made it dangerous.

Kaelen stood in the center of the old ritual chamber beneath the Academy—a place abandoned since the truce was signed, its walls carved with faded wards, its air thick with dust and forgotten power. Seraphina had found it in the Valtiel archives, a room built to contain the kind of magic that could not be spoken of in daylight.

Now, in the hour before dawn, it would serve a different purpose.

Rin had secured the perimeter, her glutton’s senses stretched to catch any approaching footsteps. Elara was at the chamber’s entrance, her books spread around her, cross-referencing the old wards with the seal’s patterns. Seraphina stood beside Kaelen, her hand in his, her face pale but resolute.
