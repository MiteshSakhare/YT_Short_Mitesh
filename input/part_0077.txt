# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
Her touch ignited the darkness within him, but at what impossible cost?

She pulled her hand back, her grin returning, but there was something fragile beneath it. "So yeah. I'm attached. Deal with it."

He didn't have words for what he felt. Gratitude, yes. Affection, perhaps. Something that might, in another life, have been the beginning of something softer. But the Demon Emperor in him whispered warnings: Attachment is a weakness. People are tools. Letting someone close is letting them see where to strike.

He pushed the voice down. It had served him well for three hundred years, but it had also left him alone on a mountain of corpses, dying with no one to mourn him.

"I'll deal with it," he said.

Rin's smile was sunrise.

Elara found him in the library three days later.

She had been avoiding him since the tribunal—or perhaps he had been avoiding her. The notebook she'd left on his bunk was tucked into his coat, its pages already worn from rereading. The seal is a marriage, she had written. And neither of them knows it yet.
