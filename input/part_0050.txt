# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
The seal erupted.

Not in response to Kaelen’s will, but in defense. A pulse of black light shot from his chest, slamming into the inquisitor’s hand and throwing him back against the judge’s bench. The masked man crumpled, his robes smoking, his breath coming in ragged gasps.

Chaos erupted. Church cadets surged to their feet, hands reaching for weapons. Demon students formed a defensive wall around Seraphina. Human students screamed and scrambled for the exits.

Kaelen stood frozen, the black light receding, the seal burning against his ribs. I didn’t do that, he thought. The seal did. It reacted to his touch. It’s protecting itself. Protecting… me?

Vex’ahlia’s voice cut through the pandemonium like a blade. She stood at the entrance to the chamber, barefoot, silver hair loose, her tarnished-gold eyes blazing with authority. Behind her, a dozen Church wardens in full battle regalia formed a wall of steel and faith.

She walked into the chamber, and the chaos subsided. Students froze. Cadets lowered their weapons. Even the demon lord judge inclined his head, a gesture of reluctant respect.

“Commander,” the masked inquisitor rasped, struggling to his feet. “The heretic attacked me. He must be—”

“He did not attack you,” Vex’ahlia interrupted. “I saw what happened. You touched a binding seal you did not understand, and it responded. That is not an attack. That is a defense mechanism.” She turned to Kaelen, her expression unreadable. “You are full of surprises, Vane.”

She walked to the center of the chamber, positioning herself between Kaelen and the judges. “The tribunal will hear from a new witness. Summon Seraphina Valtiel.”
