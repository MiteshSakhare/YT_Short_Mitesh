# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
He was considering eating in his room when a hand caught his wrist.

“Sit with me.”

Rin materialized beside him, her auburn hair somehow even wilder than before, her uniform missing two buttons. She dragged him toward a corner table tucked behind a pillar—isolated, half-hidden, the perfect place for a conversation that wasn’t meant for the crowd.

“You caused quite a stir,” she said, dropping into a chair and pulling him down beside her. Her thigh pressed against his, warm and insistent. “Everyone’s talking about the little Duke who suddenly decided to be a dragon.”

“I’m not a Duke’s son. I’m a Valtiel cousin, apparently.”

“Even better.” She stole a piece of bread from his tray and bit into it, her eyes never leaving his face. “A bastard prince with a stolen crown. That’s my favorite kind.”

He let the comment pass. “Why did you pull me over here? You could have sat with Seraphina.”

“Could have. Didn’t want to.” She leaned closer, her voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper. “She’s got plans for you, big plans. Alliances, politics, heirs.” She rolled the last word off her tongue with obvious distaste. “But I don’t care about any of that. I just want to know one thing.”

Her eyes flickered to his chest, where the seal pulsed beneath his uniform. “When you let go today, for that one second… what did it feel like?”
