# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
When three centuries of darkness awakened on his finger, one thing was certain: he was home.

He saw them before they saw him.

Seraphina stood at the Academy gates, her silver-blue hair catching the dawn, her golden eyes fixed on the horizon. Rin was beside her, her auburn hair wild, her hands clenched at her sides. Elara sat on the steps, her notebooks spread around her, but her eyes were not on the pages—they were on the borderlands, searching.

And behind them, the defenders of the Academy had gathered—students from all three factions, their uniforms mixing colors that had once been forbidden, their faces turned toward the rising sun and the figure walking out of the darkness.

Kaelen raised his hand, and the silver ring caught the light, and he saw Seraphina’s face break.
