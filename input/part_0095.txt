# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
“We did it.” He stood, Seraphina still leaning against him. “All of us.”

Rin crossed the room in three strides and threw herself at him, her arms wrapping around his neck, her face buried in his shoulder. She was shaking, he realized. The glutton demon who had never shown fear was trembling against him.

“I could feel it,” she whispered. “When the seal broke. The power. It was like the whole world was screaming.” She pulled back, her orange eyes bright. “You came back.”

“I never left.”

She kissed him—hard, desperate, tasting of salt and magic—and he let her, one arm around Seraphina, one hand in Rin’s hair, the power within him humming in answer to both of them.

When she pulled back, Elara was standing a few feet away, her arms full of books, her face flushed.

“The wards,” she said, her voice carefully controlled. “They’re holding, but the power release will have been noticed. The Church will know something happened. Valerius will move faster.”

Kaelen nodded, releasing Rin and Seraphina. He looked at the three of them—his demon heir, his glutton, his scholar—and felt the weight of what he was about to ask.

“The plan hasn’t changed. Valerius wants a heretic. I’ll give him one. But I need to know: are you with me?”

Seraphina straightened, her composure returning. “I was with you before you were born, Emperor. I’m not leaving now.”

Rin’s grin was feral. “I’m your muscle, remember? Muscles don’t run.”
