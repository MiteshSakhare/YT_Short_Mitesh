# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
Seraphina descended from the gallery with the grace of a falling star. Her gown tonight was deep crimson, her horns polished to a mirror shine, her silver-blue hair flowing like water. She stopped beside Kaelen, close enough that her arm brushed his, and inclined her head to the judges.

“Lady Seraphina,” the human judge said, her voice strained. “You are the accused’s cousin. What can you tell us about his… condition?”

Seraphina smiled—warm, open, utterly false. “Kaelen’s mana is unusual because he carries a bloodline blessing from our grandmother. A blessing that has been dormant for generations. Its awakening was… unexpected. But it is not corruption. It is not heresy. It is simply power that this Academy has not encountered before.”

She reached out and took Kaelen’s hand, lacing her fingers through his. The seal pulsed once, softly, and a faint golden light traced the lines of her palm. “If the Church wishes to test him, I will cooperate fully. But he is not a threat. He is a boy who has just discovered that he is more than he was told. And I will not allow him to be condemned for being extraordinary.”

The demon judge nodded slowly. “A bloodline blessing. That would explain the unusual resonance. The Valtiel line has produced such anomalies before.”

“Once,” the masked inquisitor spat. “Once, three hundred years ago. And that anomaly nearly destroyed the world.”
