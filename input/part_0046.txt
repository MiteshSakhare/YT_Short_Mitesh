# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
Midnights Reckoning: Seraphina Valtiel rises from the shadows, her true power unleashed.

Gaius was not in the gallery. He stood near the judge’s bench, his role undefined, his presence a question mark.

The tribunal convened with a gavel of black iron, struck once against a block of obsidian. The sound echoed through the chamber, silencing the whispers.

The judges were three: a human noblewoman with iron-gray hair and the cold eyes of a career politician; a demon lord with scales along his jaw and a perpetual sneer; and a Church inquisitor whose face was hidden behind a white mask, his voice distorted by magic.

The human judge spoke first. “Kaelen Vane, heir to the Ducal House of Vane, you stand accused of violating the Academy truce through an unprovoked display of hostile mana during a sanctioned sparring session. Additionally, the Church faction has submitted evidence of mana corruption and suspected heresy. How do you plead?”

Kaelen kept his voice steady, his posture relaxed. “Not guilty to both charges.”

The masked inquisitor leaned forward. “You deny that your mana registered as corrupt during the initial test?”
