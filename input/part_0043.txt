# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
Rin was waiting for him at the end of the hall, leaning against a pillar with her arms crossed. Her expression was unusually serious.

“She’s not going to stop the tribunal.”

“I figured.” Rin pushed off the pillar and fell into step beside him. “What’s your plan?”

“I don’t have one yet.”

“Then let me help.” She grabbed his arm, pulling him to a stop. “Seraphina will try to use this to tighten her grip on you. Gaius will try to use it to position himself as your protector. Elara will try to use it to get closer to your secrets.” Her orange eyes blazed. “I’m the only one who doesn’t want anything from you except what you’re willing to give. So let me help. Let me be your… what’s the human word? Conscience? No. Muscle. Let me be your muscle.”

He looked at her—really looked. Beneath the wild hair and the missing buttons, beneath the glutton’s hunger and the casual flirtation, there was something fierce. Something loyal.

“Why?” he asked. “Why do you want to help me?”

She was quiet for a moment, her hand still on his arm. “Because when you stood in that arena today, when you let that power out…” She swallowed, her cheeks flushing. “I’ve tasted a lot of mana. Good mana, bad mana, old mana, new mana. But yours… yours tasted like home. Like somewhere I’ve been looking for my whole life without knowing it.” She met his eyes, her gaze unwavering. “I don’t know what that means. But I’m not going to let anyone take that away before I figure it out.”
