# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
When darkness birthed the Lion, truth became a blade forged in blood and betrayal.

He raised his hand, and behind him, the great engines of war began to move.

"You want truth?" Aldric's voice was a snarl. "Here is truth: the truce is dead. The Academy is mine. And you—you will burn with it."

Kaelen stood motionless as the king's hand began to fall.

And then the world exploded.

It was not fire. It was not darkness. It was light—pure, blinding, a column of silver that erupted from the Academy walls and struck the ground between Kaelen and the king's horse, sending the animal rearing, throwing Aldric from the saddle.

Seraphina stood on the walls, her hands raised, her silver-blue hair wild, her golden eyes blazing with power. Beside her, Rin had her dagger drawn, her glutton demons spreading along the parapets like shadows given form. Elara was at the gates, her sword forgotten, a scroll of ancient wards in her hands, her voice rising in a chant that made the very stones of the Academy hum.
