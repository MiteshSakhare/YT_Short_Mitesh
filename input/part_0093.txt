# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
When seraphic whispers whispered my name to the Demon Emperors throne.

She leaned in and kissed him—soft, slow, a goodbye and a beginning all at once. When she pulled back, there were tears in her eyes, but her voice was steady.

“Take it off.”

She placed her hand over the bloodline lock on his collar. Her palm blazed with golden light—the same light that had bound him to her family for three hundred years. The seal screamed. The frozen ocean surged against its chains. And Seraphina pulled.

The pain was unlike anything Kaelen had felt. It wasn’t physical—it was deeper, older, the tearing of something that had become part of his very soul. He heard Seraphina cry out, felt her grip on his hand convulse, and then—

The lock broke.

The collar fell from his neck, clattering to the stone floor. And the power that had been sealed for three hundred years erupted.

Black light flooded the chamber, cold and absolute, frost spreading across the walls, the ceiling, the floor. Elara gasped, her books flying open, pages ripping. Seraphina was thrown back, caught against a pillar by the force of the wave.

And in the center of it all, Kaelen Dragomir—once Demon Emperor, now something else—opened his eyes.
