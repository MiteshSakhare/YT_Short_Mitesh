# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
“You know what I am,” he said.

“I know what you were.” She folded her hands on the desk, and for a moment, her fingers traced a sigil in the air—a pattern he recognized. The same pattern that bound his seal. “The Demon Emperor. The man who broke three realms and then broke himself trying to hold them. I’ve studied your campaigns. Your strategies. Your…” She smiled, thin and cold. “…your failures.”

“I’m sure you have.”

“You died three hundred years ago. Your empire crumbled. Your name became a curse. And now you’re back, wearing the flesh of a Valtiel half-blood, bound to the very bloodline you betrayed.” Her eyes glittered. “The Church should destroy you. You know that. You’re a relic of a war we’ve spent centuries trying to forget.”

“But you’re not going to destroy me.”

She leaned back, studying him. “No. Because the Church is not what it pretends to be. The Inquisition hunts heretics, yes. But the heresy we fear most is the one inside our own ranks. And lately…” She pulled a folded parchment from her sleeve and slid it across the desk. “Lately, there have been signs. Prophecies. A king who will rise not from the demon lords or the human thrones, but from the cracks between them. A king who will break the truce—or remake it.”

Kaelen unfolded the parchment. The writing was ancient, the ink faded, but the words were clear:

When the twice-crowned king returns,

The bonds of blood shall break and burn.

Three factions fall, one world shall rise,

And truth shall wear a heretic’s eyes.

He looked up. “You think this is about me.”
