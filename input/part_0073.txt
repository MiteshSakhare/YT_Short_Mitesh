# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
Is your anchor the source of freedom, or its most deadly curse?

“What?” His voice was hoarse, the memories still echoing in his skull.

“Connection.” Morwen’s red eyes held his. “The seal no longer binds you to Seraphina’s bloodline. It binds you to her. To Rin, who has tasted your power and made it part of herself. To the human girl who knows your secrets and chooses to stay. To everyone who touches you, who marks you, who wants you.” She stepped closer, her voice dropping to a whisper meant only for him. “You wanted to be alone. You built an empire to prove you didn’t need anyone. But now, you are surrounded. And the more you let them in, the stronger you become.”

She pulled back, her expression hardening. “The formal evaluation is complete. My report will state that Kaelen Vane is not a threat to the truce. He is, however, a variable that cannot be controlled by any single faction.” She looked at the observers—the demon lords, the human representative, Vex’ahlia. “If you try to cage him, the cage will break. If you try to use him, the hand that reaches for him will burn. The only choice you have is whether to stand beside him or be swept away when he rises.”
