# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
Will I unleash the darkness within him, or will he claim me as his own?

She pulled back, her expression shifting from warmth to calculation. "The demon lords have sent their evaluator. She arrives tomorrow. Her name is Lady Morwen, and she is old—older than the truce, older than the Academy, older than almost anything in this world. She served your original empire, three hundred years ago. She knows what you were."

Kaelen's blood went cold. "She knew me?"

"She knew the Demon Emperor. And she has spent three centuries waiting for his return." Seraphina's golden eyes were serious. "If she decides you are the same man who abandoned her mistress, she will destroy you. Not with power—she doesn't need power. She'll destroy you with truth. She'll tell the Academy exactly what you are, what you did, who you betrayed. And no limiter, no truce, no bloodline bond will save you from that."

She turned to leave, then paused. "Tomorrow, you meet your past. I suggest you decide, before then, who you want to be when it arrives."

She walked away, her gown trailing behind her, her footsteps echoing in the empty corridor.

Kaelen stood alone, the collar heavy around his neck, Elara's notebook pressed against his chest, the prophecy burning in his pocket.

Twice-crowned king, he thought. Once for what I was. Once for what I'm becoming.
