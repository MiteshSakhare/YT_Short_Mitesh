# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
He considered the question. “Like remembering a language I’d forgotten.”

“Mm.” She reached out and placed her palm flat against his chest, over his heart. Her hand was small, her fingers calloused, her touch electric. “And what language is that, Emperor? The one that tastes like midnight and old empires?”

The seal pulsed against her palm, and she gasped softly, her cheeks flushing. “Oh,” she breathed. “Oh, that’s… that’s good.”

He caught her wrist, pulling her hand away. “Rin.”

“Kaelen.” She smiled, unrepentant. “You’re going to need allies who don’t want to put you in a cage or a breeding program. I’m offering. No politics, no strings. Just…” She leaned in, her lips brushing his ear. “…hunger. Pure and simple. You feed me, I’ll fight for you. And maybe, sometimes, I’ll let you feed on me.”

She pulled back, her grin wolfish. “Think about it.”

Before he could respond, a shadow fell over the table.

Gaius stood there, his pale blue eyes fixed on Kaelen, his expression unreadable. He was dressed in civilian clothes—no Inquisition insignia, no weapons visible—but the threat radiated off him like heat from a forge.

“Vane. A word.”

Rin’s hand moved to her hip, where a concealed dagger was likely hidden. “He’s busy.”

“He’s in danger,” Gaius said flatly. “The Church faction is convening an emergency tribunal. Captain Valerius is pushing for your immediate detention and interrogation. They’re claiming your display was a violation of the truce—an act of aggression by a known heretic.”
