# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
“Well,” she said, stepping to his side, her hand finding his. “It seems my cousin has finally decided to stop sleeping through his destiny.”

She squeezed his fingers, and the seal pulsed in response—not in pain, but in recognition.

For the first time since he’d woken in this body, Kaelen felt something that might have been hope.

Or it might have been the beginning of something far more dangerous.

The silence that followed Kaelen’s display was not peaceful. It was the silence of a held breath, of a hundred hearts calculating, of the fragile architecture of the Academy’s peace groaning under a new weight.

The demon girl with the ram’s horns was released. The injured Church cadet was carried to the healers. Students dispersed in clusters, their whispers sharp as broken glass. And through it all, Kaelen stood at the center of the arena, Seraphina’s hand still clasped around his, feeling the seal thrum like a plucked harp string.

I showed too much, he thought, watching the Church cadets retreat with looks that ranged from fear to hatred to something more clinical. But hiding was no longer an option.

“You’re an idiot,” Seraphina murmured beside him, her voice light but her grip iron. “A magnificent, reckless idiot.”

“Your assessment is noted.”

“You’ve just painted a target on your back the size of the Academy.” She released his hand, but stayed close, her shoulder brushing his. “Every faction will want you now. The Church will demand you be tested, catalogued, controlled. The human nobles will see you as either a weapon or a threat. And my demon lords…” She smiled, thin and sharp. “They’ll want to know why a half-blood nobody just manifested power that rivals a high lord’s.”
