# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
From the southern gallery, Rin cupped her hands around her mouth. “I resent being called ‘unwanted’!”

Vorg pulled her back into her seat, but the damage was done. Laughter rippled through the demon section. Even some humans smirked.

The human judge struck her gavel. “Order! The gallery will remain silent!”

The masked inquisitor stood slowly, his white robes pooling around him like spilled milk. “The accused raises a valid point. Marks alone do not constitute heresy. But the black resonance… that is another matter.” He walked around the bench, approaching Kaelen with measured steps. “The Church has records of black mana appearing only three times in the last thousand years. Each time, it heralded a cataclysm. Wars. Plagues. The rise of demon lords who nearly consumed the world.” He stopped a pace away, his masked face tilted down toward Kaelen. “What are you, boy? What sleeps inside you?”

Kaelen met the blank white mask without flinching. “I am Kaelen Vane. What sleeps inside me is my inheritance. Nothing more.”

The inquisitor’s head tilted. “Inheritance from whom? The Vane line has no history of black mana. The Valtiel line has no history of black mana. You are an anomaly. A deviation. And the Church does not tolerate deviations that threaten the peace.”

He reached out, his gloved hand hovering over Kaelen’s chest—directly above the seal. “I will know what you are. One way or another.”
