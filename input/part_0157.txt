# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
When the darkness that destroyed worlds was unleashed upon him, he refused to perish.

He bowed his head. “That’s the world you’re building. I was wrong to fight it. I was wrong to try to tear it down.”

The chamber was silent. Then Mara spoke, her voice soft behind her mask. “What do you propose we do with him?”

Kaelen stepped forward. “Let him live. Let him face what he’s done. Not with a sword, but with a trial. Let the people he hurt decide his fate. That’s what we’re building—a world where justice isn’t just the victor’s judgment.”

Lady Ashford’s jaw tightened. “He killed Church priests. He nearly started a war. You expect us to simply—”

“I expect you to be better than him.” Kaelen’s voice was calm, but there was steel beneath it. “He came here to die. He wanted to be a martyr, a symbol, something for his followers to rally around. If you execute him, you give him exactly what he wants. If you give him a trial, you show the world that this council is not about revenge. It’s about justice.”

Lord Malachar nodded slowly. “The boy speaks sense. Let him stand trial. Let his crimes be known. And let his followers see that we are not the monsters they were told we were.”
