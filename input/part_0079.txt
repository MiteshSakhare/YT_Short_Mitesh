# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
When whispers of your past ignite a prophecy, will you rise to reclaim your eternal fate?

"I didn't ask for any of this," he said.

"No." Elara reached across the table, her fingers brushing his. "But you're not pushing it away either. That's new, isn't it? The Demon Emperor would have crushed anyone who got too close. But you… you let Rin sleep beside you. You let Seraphina claim you. You let me give you this." She tapped the notebook in his coat. "You're changing."

"Maybe I'm just getting better at wearing masks."

"Maybe." Her fingers tightened around his. "Or maybe you're finally letting yourself want something other than power."

He should have pulled away. Should have retreated behind the walls that had kept him alive for three centuries. But her hand was warm, and her eyes were honest, and somewhere deep in the frozen ocean, something cracked—not breaking, but melting.

"What do you want, Elara?"

She was quiet for a long moment, her thumb tracing circles on the back of his hand. "I want to understand you. I want to be close enough to see what you become. I want…" She looked down at their joined hands, a flush creeping up her neck. "I want to be part of it. Whatever it is. Whatever you're building."
