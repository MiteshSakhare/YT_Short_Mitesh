# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
Elara clutched her books tighter, but her voice was steady. “I want to see what you become. All of it.”

He looked at them—three women who had chosen him, who had risked everything for him, who saw something in him that he was only beginning to understand.

“Then let’s give them a show.”

Above them, in the Church quarter, Captain Valerius was already assembling his forces.

The power surge had been detected within minutes—a spike of black mana that registered on every monitoring crystal in the Academy. The demon lords were in an uproar. The human nobles were demanding answers. And Valerius stood at the head of his Inquisition squad, his scarred face alight with righteous fury.

“The heretic has broken his limiter,” he announced to the gathered cadets. “He has revealed his true nature. The demon faction has been harboring a weapon of mass destruction, and now that weapon is loose. We will move immediately. We will secure the heretic. And we will purge this Academy of the corruption that threatens it.”

He raised his sword, its blade gleaming with celestial light. “For the Celestial Seers. For the truce. For the future of all humanity!”

The cadets cheered. Behind them, the Church’s war machines hummed to life, their silver sigils blazing with stored power.

But in the shadows of the corridor, a figure in gray watched, her tarnished-gold eyes tracking Valerius’s movements.

Vex’ahlia did not cheer. She did not draw her weapon. She simply waited, her hand closed around the small silver key she had given away, and wondered which way the storm would break.
