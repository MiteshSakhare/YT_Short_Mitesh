# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
Seal broken, darkness unleashed: His name was the only sound that could set her free.

“I’m here,” he said, holding her. “I’m here.”

“You were gone so long.” Her voice was muffled, her face buried in his shoulder. “Three days. Three days, and I thought—”

“I know.” He stroked her hair, feeling her tremble. “I’m sorry.”

She pulled back, her face wet, her golden eyes blazing. “If you ever do that again—”

“I won’t.” He smiled, tired but genuine. “The darkness is settled. The Oracle is… beginning her own journey. The fortress is being rebuilt. There’s no war, Seraphina. Not anymore.”

She stared at him, searching his face for the lie. And then she kissed him, fierce and desperate, and he kissed her back, the silver ring warm between them, the dawn painting them both in gold.

When they broke apart, Rin was there, her grin shaky, her eyes bright. “You’re late.”

“I had to save the world. It took longer than expected.”

“Next time, save the world faster.” She punched his arm, then pulled him into a hug, her face pressed against his neck. “You taste like dawn,” she whispered. “Like something new.”
