# Part 5: The Heretic’s Gambit

NARRATOR:
Seraphinas eyes ignited with hellfire as she whispered a single, chilling word.

Valerius’s face went pale. He looked at the watching students—demon, human, and Church alike—and saw the political trap closing around him. He sheathed his sword with a snarl.

“This isn’t over, Vane,” he spat. “Corrupt mana doesn’t lie. And neither does the Inquisition.”

He turned and marched back down the corridor, his squad falling in behind him. The crowd began to disperse, the tension draining into whispered speculation.

Kaelen exhaled slowly. His heart was pounding—not from fear, but from the effort of containing the power that had surged against the seal when Valerius drew his sword. For a moment, he had wanted to unleash it. To show them all what a true heretic looked like.

He looked at Gaius. “You saved me. Why?”
