# Part 2: The Serpent’s Welcome

NARRATOR:
Demons didnt wear beauty; darkness wore them.

Behind her stood two other demon students: a towering young man with bull-like horns and a perpetual scowl, and a slender girl with moth-like antennae who watched Kaelen with unnerving stillness.

“I don’t believe we’ve been introduced,” Kaelen said, his voice mild.

The golden-eyed girl smiled. It was a predator’s smile. “No. But I know you, Emperor.” She stepped closer, close enough that the scent of roses—the same scent from the letter—wreathed around him. “The question is: do you remember me?”

The seal in his chest pulsed again, sharper this time. A flicker of a memory, not his own but from this body’s original, fragmented soul. A garden. A demon child with broken horns. A promise whispered in the dark.

He kept his expression placid. “I’m afraid you have me at a disadvantage, my lady.”

Her smile widened. “Good. I prefer it that way for now. Let’s see how long the mighty Demon Emperor can wear that sheep’s clothing before the wolves come to play.” She reached out and traced a single finger down his cheek, her nail leaving a faint, stinging line. “I am Seraphina Valtiel. Heiress to the Demon Lords’ council. And you, Kaelen Vane, owe me a debt older than your borrowed skin.”
