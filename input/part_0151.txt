# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
When darkness whispers secrets, will you answer?

He reached the chamber just as the figure emerged from the shadows.

The scarred captain looked nothing like the man who had led the purge. His uniform was gone, replaced by armor of black iron, unmarked, unadorned. His face was thinner, the scar that crossed it pulled tight, his eyes burning with a fervor that had curdled into madness. In his hand, he carried a blade that drank the light—a sword of celestial silver, but corrupted, its edge weeping shadow.

“Heretic,” Valerius breathed, his voice a rasp. “I knew you’d come.”

Kaelen stopped in the center of the chamber, the silver ring warm against his skin. “You shouldn’t have come back, Valerius. The council would have given you a trial.”

“A trial?” Valerius laughed, a broken, hollow sound. “You mean a circus. A spectacle where the demons and their whore-queen would parade my failures and call it justice.” He raised the corrupted sword, its edge hissing. “I am the justice. I am the faith that your heresy tried to burn.”

Kaelen moved without thinking, the power within him surging, black light meeting black steel. The impact shook the chamber, dust raining from the ceiling, the wards that Valerius had cut screaming back to life. Valerius was strong—stronger than he should have been, the corruption in his blade feeding him power that was not his own.
